About

I started programming around 6 years ago, fueled by a passion for developing games that stemmed from my love of playing them since childhood. Initially self-taught in C++, I soon joined a group of friends to create games using Unity, despite our collective inexperience in game development.

During this period, not only did I learn C#, but I also explored Windows Application development using both C++ (with the ImGui library) and C# (Windows Forms). However, I realized Unity’s limitations and felt Unreal Engine was too cumbersome for my team’s needs. Consequently, we embarked on building our own engine.

At that point, I was familiar with OpenGL, but we opted for Vulkan due to its extensive developer control and multithreading capabilities. To guide our engine’s architecture, I studied “The Cherno"’s Game Engine series and read Jason Gregory’s “Game Engine Architecture” â€” a highly recommended book.

After a year of intense development, we created an engine with “TsarEd,” an editor capable of rendering complex meshes, supporting Instanced Rendering, Directional & Point Lights, C# .Net Core using Mono for Scripting (with plans to switch to C++ scripting), Box2D for physics, miniaudio for audio, and ImGui for UI. This project is still under development.

In early 2022, we started developing “The Meaning”, a psychological horror game which was later released in Q4 2022 made using Unity.

Our current project, “Eternal Vigil: Crystal Defender” is a Tower-Defense, Strategy & Action game. The objective is to defend a crystal from waves of monsters, utilizing a wide range of weapons and towers.

Throughout my programming journey, I’ve also engaged with various tools, APIs, SDKs, and frameworks, some for projects and others during my university studies.
